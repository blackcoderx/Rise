How does the device works

Input Stage:
The patient will enter his name, age, and hospital ID if he is a member of the hospital. The ID will help the backend system connect the electronic health record in the prediction process. After entering the necessary credentials, he will describe how he feels using text or images on the device. The images will be illustrations depicting various known symptoms. If he doesn't see an illustration that matches how he feels in the carousel of images, he can enter text instead.

Processing Stage:
The device transmits comprehensive patient records, including vital details like name, age, and hospital ID, which are linked to electronic health records. The AI backend uses this data to predict potential illnesses or diseases. The algorithm, designed and continuously learning, analyzes the data to generate insightful predictions. The AI's ability to recognize patterns, correlate symptoms, and draw from a vast knowledge base enables healthcare professionals to make informed decisions.

Output stage:
After the AI generates insights from predictive analysis, providing a comprehensive overview of potential diseases based on a patient's symptoms. These insights are then transmitted to the nurse, enabling targeted checks and assessments. For example, hypertension can be identified as a concern, allowing for additional diagnostic tests and physical examinations. The AI also acts as a supportive partner, providing contextual information and guiding decision-making. This collaborative approach ensures informed and timely care, resulting in a more precise, efficient, and personalized approach to patient care.

Why is my device the solution?

Easy symptom recognition:
The system will provide a user-friendly interface for healthcare providers to identify and assess patient symptoms, aiding accurate and timely diagnoses. By using artificial intelligence, the device can quickly analyze symptoms and provide healthcare providers with valuable information, enabling early detection and timely treatment of diseases or illnesses.

Reduce deaths from delayed diagnosis:
By improving symptom recognition and enabling efficient communication, the solution will help healthcare providers quickly identify and treat critical conditions, reducing deaths from delayed diagnosis.

Enhanced user experience:
The system will have an intuitive design that ensures easy navigation and access to information, promoting a positive user experience. Continuous improvement through user testing and feedback: User testing will gather feedback for ongoing improvement, ensuring that the solution meets the needs and preferences of healthcare providers and patients.

Secure data storage:
The solution will implement robust measures to securely store patient information, complying with regulations and standards. Collaboration with healthcare professionals: The solution will be developed in close collaboration with healthcare professionals, incorporating their input to align with their needs and workflows.
